## Short Impact Notes For Presenter

- "BSL has legal recognition, but that does not magically put an interpreter in every appointment."
- "The problem is not only hearing loss. BSL is a language access issue: BSL is not signed English, and written English is not an adequate substitute for many users."
- "Housing damp and mould is a strong demo because it is concrete: a tenant reports a hazard, the service has policy obligations, and a record matters."
- "The advocacy layer is the serious bit: policy card, follow-up question, and exportable record."
- "The local-first argument is strongest when we say exactly what leaves the camera path: landmark events and accepted text, not raw video."

## Claims To Keep Out Of The Pitch

- Do not say the City of London figures represent all London boroughs.
- Do not use the GBP 80m-100m health-access cost as a housing cost.
- Do not imply Awaab's Law applies to every private rental unless the specific tenure is checked.
- Do not say the policy card is legal advice.
- Do not say on-device ElevenLabs is available unless the official activation flow has succeeded.
- Do not say Signbridge is trained on BOBSL or CSLR2 unless the data licence and training path are real.
