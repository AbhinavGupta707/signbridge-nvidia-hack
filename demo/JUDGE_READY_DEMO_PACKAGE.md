## Non-Negotiable Demo Claims

Say:

- "Signbridge helps in the gap when no qualified human interpreter is available."
- "This hackathon demo recognises a constrained, rehearsed housing-repair vocabulary."
- "The Deaf user sees and confirms the recognised meaning before it becomes part of the record."
- "No raw video leaves the room; the record is consent-gated."
- "The policy and question cards are source-backed, not legal advice."
- "Production requires Deaf-led co-design, broader consented data, and formal governance."

Do not say:

- "We translate BSL generally."
- "This replaces interpreters."
- "This is safe for clinical diagnosis, legal proceedings, police interviews, or binding decisions."
- "ElevenLabs on-device is guaranteed" unless the official event/sponsor activation has been verified.
- "The model understands all signers" or "works across regional BSL variation."

## Winning Narrative

Signbridge is not a gesture demo. It is a local-first public-service communication tool: sign intent to spoken English, speech to captions, policy support, a practical follow-up question, and an appointment record the user controls.

The judge should leave with three ideas:

- The privacy argument is real because the sensitive vision, language, voice, retrieval, and record stack runs locally.
- The impact argument is real because BSL users face documented barriers in public services, and damp and mould repairs are a concrete London public-service workflow with sourceable obligations.
- The ethics argument is real because we are explicit about limits: augmentation, not replacement.
